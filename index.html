About

Where Technology Meets
Conservation Botany

I am the Collections Specialist at Montgomery Botanical Center in Coral Gables, Florida, where I manage and maintain the institution's maps and GIS database. I lead a small rotating team focused on garden inventory, mapping, utility locating, software development, and database management, building interactive maps and tools that help visiting researchers target species across our 120-acre property.

My work sits at the intersection of geospatial technology and plant conservation. At MBC, I modernized the inventory and plant record database processes by implementing a mobile-based data collection solution that compressed months of fieldwork into a 72-day window — then collaborated with BGB developers to automate data upload, turning months of effort into seconds of systematic review. I have also established new Base and Rover GNSS reference points throughout the property to improve GIS infrastructure and productivity.

Beyond my collections work, I contribute to research at the Lab for Conservation & Sustainable Horticulture at Florida International University, and I am co-author on publications addressing the taxonomy of Cuban endemic palms and the rediscovery and restoration of Sabal miamiensis, a palm once listed as Extinct in the Wild. I am also developing deep learning computer vision models for plant identification from aerial imagery — work aimed at enabling ecological census of species at landscape scale.

Projects & Works

From Databases to
Deep Learning

🗺️

Database Modernization at MBC

Implemented a mobile-based solution for data collection across a 120-acre property, compressing months of inventory work into a 72-day window. Collaborated with BGB developers to automate data upload after systematic review.

📡

GIS Infrastructure Improvement

Established new Base and Rover GNSS equipment with calibrated reference points throughout MBC property, reducing labor hours and increasing spatial accuracy and productivity for all mapping operations.

🤖

Deep Learning for Plant ID

Developing computer vision models for plant identification within aerial imagery to enable ecological census of species at landscape scale — supporting conservation practices, population dynamics studies, and sustainable land use.

🌿

Borassus aethiopum Germination

Research project analyzing the effects of thermal inputs on germination factors in Borassus aethiopum, with the goal of improving recruitment, sustainability, and economic growth for native communities.

🌱

Cycad Conservation with MBC & UU

Collaborative research with the University of Utah's Dr. Irene Terry investigating endangered cycad pollination mechanisms, phenology, mutualism, and adaptation within an ex situ garden setting.

🌴

Sabal miamiensis Research

Contributing to the rediscovery, documentation, and restoration of Sabal miamiensis — a palm once listed as Extinct in the Wild. Co-author on publications describing new wild populations and ongoing restoration efforts in Miami-Dade County.

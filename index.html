About

Where Technology Meets
Conservation Botany

I am the Collections Specialist at Montgomery Botanical Center in Coral Gables, Florida, where I manage the institution's maps, GIS database, and garden inventory systems. My work sits at the intersection of geospatial technology and plant conservation — I build interactive tools and field workflows that help researchers locate and study species across the MBC living collection.

My scientific journey began with an internship at Redland Ahead, a controlled-environment agriculture farm in South Florida. At MBC, I was introduced to conservation research through Dr. Amir Khoddamzadeh and the Conservation Fellowship program, and have since advanced through several roles while collaborating with researchers on cycad pollination ecology, coyote dietary studies, palm taxonomy, and SEM-based floral anatomy. These experiences broadened my perspective and gave me the confidence to pursue my own independent research.

That research centers on Borassus aethiopum, the African Palmyra palm, whose germination ecology I am exploring at the intersection of thermal biology and conservation. I am also developing computer vision and robotic surveying tools for automated plant identification in endangered pine rockland habitats — work that combines my GIS background with deep learning to address real gaps in biodiversity monitoring.

Focal Species

Research Organisms

My independent research centers on Borassus aethiopum, a robust African palm with untapped potential for both ecological study and community livelihood. Collaborative projects span additional palm and cycad taxa at Montgomery Botanical Center.

Borassus aethiopum

African Palmyra Palm · Rônier

A dioecious fan palm of sub-Saharan African dry savannas, valued for timber, fruit, sap, and fiber. Distributed across Benin, Cameroon, Nigeria, Uganda, and more than 49 additional localities. Despite IUCN Least Concern status globally, populations are threatened by shifting agriculture and intentional harvest. At MBC, I study thermal inputs on germination with the goal of improving recruitment, sustainability, and economic growth for native communities that depend on this palm.

IUCN — Least Concern

Cycas micronesica

Micronesian Cycad

An endangered cycad native to Micronesia, Palau, and Guam, threatened by the invasive cycad aulacaspis scale. MBC maintains one of the most significant ex situ collections of this species, and I contribute to collaborative research on its pollination ecology and reproductive biology with Dr. Irene Terry at the University of Utah.

Endangered

Coccothrinax crinita

Old Man Palm

A rare Cuban endemic recognized for the distinctive fibrous sheath covering its trunk. Native to western Cuba and critically threatened by habitat loss, it is maintained in cultivation at MBC and several other botanical gardens worldwide. I contributed SEM and macro photography of floral anatomy to a collaborative nomenclatural study of this species.

Critically Endangered

Sabal miamiensis

Miami Palm

A critically imperiled palm endemic to Miami-Dade County, listed as Extinct in the Wild since 1997 until wild populations were confirmed in 2024. I support ongoing field surveys and restoration efforts at Montgomery Botanical Center as part of a collaborative team led by Daniel Tucker.

Projects & Investigations

From Germination Ecology
to Robotic Surveying

🌴

Thermal Effects on Borassus Germination

Designing controlled experiments to quantify how temperature regimes affect germination rate and seedling vigor in Borassus aethiopum. The goal is to develop propagation protocols that can improve recruitment both in botanical gardens and across West African communities that rely on this palm.

🤖

Quadruped Robot for Pine Rockland Surveys

Proposed deployment of a Unitree Go2 X quadruped robot equipped with 4D LiDAR and HD camera for autonomous plant identification in South Florida's pine rocklands. The pipeline pairs a fine-tuned ResNet-50 classifier with a YOLOv8 real-time detector, targeting understory species that are difficult to census by drone.

GIAR Proposal — Sigma Xi
🗺️

MBC Inventory Modernization

Replaced a legacy paper-based inventory system with a mobile data collection workflow, compressing months of fieldwork into 72 working days. Collaborated with BGB developers to automate data upload, yielding cleaner databases and enabling new types of collections research.

📡

GNSS Infrastructure Upgrade

Established new Base and Rover GNSS equipment with calibrated reference points across MBC, increasing spatial accuracy for all mapping operations and reducing the labor required for routine survey work.

🧬

Cycas micronesica Pollination Ecology

Contributing to a long-term collaboration with Dr. Irene Terry (University of Utah) examining pollination mechanisms, phenology, and weevil mutualism in Cycas micronesica accessions held at Montgomery Botanical Center.

📷

Floral Anatomy of Coccothrinax crinita

Produced macro and scanning electron micrographs of reproductive structures for the rare Cuban old man palm, supporting a nomenclatural study led by Dr. Javier Francisco-Ortega and published in Kew Bulletin (2026).
